General Problem

Not enough college students compost. Despite overconsumption and overproduction of garbage being a large detriment to the environment, composting is not a common practice amongst full-time college students living on or near campus.

Insight

User Research

To gain a better understanding of the daily habits related to students’ composting behaviors, we recruited five undergraduate students to participate in our interviews. The interviews took place in the home of each participant, during the daytime after the participant has made a meal. This is the best time to observe how participants handle organic food waste, and for us to ask follow up questions based on their behavior.

Research Findings

Six major themes surrounding composting activities: previous knowledge, factors for and against, feelings about composting, external motivations and amount of food waste produced.

Persona Development

Sheila was developed based on synthesized notes from user research.

Sheila

Picture of Sheila

"I compost when there is an opportunity to, and it's convenient. I think it's good for the environment, but I won't go out of my way to compost."

Sheila is a junior at Cornell and lives in a shared apartment complex. Education is currently her top priority, followed by her social life. Sheila cares about how she presents herself to the world and makes an effort to maintain a reputation for being smart. She passively supports various causes that she believes are good for society and wants to be recognized for doing so.

Sheila currently has little incentive to take out the trash unless it’s absolutely necessary. For this reason, the trash will pile up in Sheila’s kitchen until it reaches a point of discomfort.

Sheila believes in the benefits of composting and thinks it’s cool. She feels cool when composting and likes being observed by her peers while doing it.

Her attitudes are highly influenced by people within her social circle; she is more likely to support a cause if others within her social network are also actively engaged.

Specific Problem

It is too inconvenient for full-time undergraduate students to compost regularly.
