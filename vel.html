Moon Phases in Virtual Reality

This project explores the effects of two new user interactions within a Virtual Reality environment through the recruitment of 30 participants for experimental lab sessions.

The 'Phases of the Moon' VR Environment

This environment aims to educate users on the different phases of the moon. Users are able to interact with the Moon in ways that include dragging into different phases and scaling in and out.

Our research examines emotions and attitudes associated with each scaling interaction.

Findings

Preference for Button Press

Overall, our users preferred the button press interaction. On average, users were more frustrated with using an arm stretching motion to scale.

Graph depicting frustration levels

However, after accounting for order effects, we were able to identify noticeable differences in enjoyment and control levels. Overall, users felt higher levels of enjoyment and lower levels of control when the button press interaction was used first. Because the button press interaction is easier to use, and this led to higher levels of enjoyment. This which suggests the need for improved tutorials that will ease users into the environment. Using the button press interaction first also led to feeling less in control when transitioning to an arms to stretch interaction; this suggests that the arms to stretch interaction needs improvement.

Graph depicting order effects

Users need better training and more instructions on how to operate within the environment.

Users need a better indication of the degree of scaling applied when using their arms to stretch.

Persona development

Our findings reveal four reoccurring types of users: curious, fixed, enthusiastic and experienced. We created four personas created to represent each theme.

Curious Caroline

"When I'm using my arms to stretch, I really feel like I'm a part of the environment."

Caroline is curious, expressive, engaged, intelligent and adaptable. She prefers the button press interaction because it offers more control. However, her wit and openness to trying new things allow her to quickly adapt in new situations. Caroline finds the arms to stretch interaction fun, but chooses to use the button press more due to it's ease of use.

Fixed Frank

"Using arms to stretch is confusing and requires too much effort"

Frank is fixed, experienced and efficient. In contrast to Caroline, he is an experienced gamer who views the Moon Phase environment as a game rather than an educational tool. He prefers the button press over arms to stretch because he finds it more intuitive, and similar to the video games he is used to playing. He feels uncomfortable with adapting to a new arms to stretch feature. Frank is very Fixed in his habits.

Enthusiastic Emily

"Is there more to it? It was over too fast!"

Emily is enthusiastic, eager and educated. She is the only person who prefers arms stretching to button press. She loves having fun with people who have passion about what they do and experiencing new things. Emily finds the arms to stretch interaction immersive and intuitive.

Experienced Eric

"I like being in control and getting tasks done fast."

Eric is engaged, experienced and highly opinionated. He is very experienced with both video games and VR. Despite the fact that Eric prefers button press over arms to stretch, he sees potential in the arms stretching interaction and considers it to be very fun. However, he provides several suggestions for improvements.
