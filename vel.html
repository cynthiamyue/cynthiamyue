This project explores the effects of two new user interactions within a Virtual Reality environment through the recruitment of 30 participants for experimental lab sessions.

The 'Phases of the Moon' VR Environment

This environment aims to educate users on the different phases of the moon. Users are able to interact with the Moon in ways that include dragging into different phases and scaling in and out.

This research examines emotions and attitudes associated with each scaling interaction.

Findings

Preference for Button Press

Overall, our users preferred the button press interaction. On average, users were more frustrated with using an arms stretching motion to scale.

Graph depicting frustration levels

However, after accounting for order effects, we were able to identify noticeable differences in enjoyment and control levels. Overall, users felt higher levels of enjoyment and lower levels of control when the button press was used first. Because the button press interaction is easier to use, and this led to higher levels of enjoyment, which suggests a need for an improved tutorials to ease users into the environment. This also led to feeling less in control once the arms to strech method was being used, which suggests the need for improvements for this interaction.

Graph depicting order effects

Users need better training and more instructions on how to operate within the environment.

Users need a better indication of the degree of scaling applied when using their arms to stretch.

Persona development

Our findings reveal four reoccurring types of users: curious, fixed, enthusiastic and experienced. We created four personas created to represent four recurring themes identified in our lab sessions

Curious Caroline

Caroline was created from data of 4 participants. She is curious, expressive, engaged, intelligent, and adaptable woman who prefers button press because it offers more control. Her openness to trying new things allow her to learn how to navigate new situations quickly; however, she gets frustrated if she does not understand how it works. Therefore, she chose button press over arms stretching.

"When I'm using my arms to stretch, I really feel like I'm a part of the environment."

Fixed Frank

Frank was created from data of 6 participants. He is indifferent, experienced and efficient. In contrast to Caroline, he is an experienced gamer so he viewed Moon phase project as a game rather than treating it as an educational tool. The main reason that he chose button press over arms stretching was that he believed button press is so much more intuitive to him. He explained that he was familiar with using abilities by pressing button in games so he did not want to change the way he controlled the environment. This is why we decided to call him fixed Frank.

"Using arms to stretch is confusing and requires too much effort"

Enthusiastic Emily

Emily was created from data of 3 participants. She is enthusiastic, eager and educated. She is the only person who prefers arms stretching to button press. She loves having fun with people who have passion about what they do and trying out new things that she has not experienced before. After trying out arms stretching function, she called it immersive and intuitive. Although she mentioned that she liked the idea of using arms to stretch the environment, she suggested improvements because she saw potential in it.

"Is there more to it? It was over too fast!"

Experienced Eric

Eric was created from data of 6 participants. He is engaged, experienced, and opinionated. He is very experienced in both games and VR. He may seem very similar to Frank, but he has more experience in VR, so he had many suggestions for improvements. Also, despite the fact that he preferred button press over arms stretching, he saw potential in the arms stretching function by calling it fun. However, he suggested that it needs lots of improvements to make it less confusing and uncomfortable.

"I like being in control and getting tasks done fast."
